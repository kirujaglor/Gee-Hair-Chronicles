Excellent Natural Hairstyles for Black Men

There are many versatile hairstyles for black men that come in many shapes and forms. From looking your best in formal meetings, to a friend’s party, you must choose a hairdo that you are comfortable with! However, each time you settle for a hairstyle, ask your stylist how much daily maintenance you require. Remember, hair differs from an individual to another while each style requires a specific type of maintenance or care. As you strive to look your best, here is a highlight of some trendy and popular hairstyles you can borrow an idea from.

  • Fade with Natural Texture:

    • A fade haircut paired with showcasing natural hair texture can create a modern and trendy look for black men. This style features short, faded sides that gradually blend into longer, textured hair on top. It offers a sleek and polished appearance while still allowing the natural hair to shine through.

  • Twisted Curls:

    • For black men with curly hair, twisting the curls can create a defined and stylish appearance. This style involves twisting sections of hair to enhance the curl pattern and add texture. Twisted curls can be worn loose for a casual look or styled into a more polished finish for formal occasions.

  • Afro:

    • The Afro is a legendary and iconic hairstyle for men. It became widely popular in the 80s, and its cultural significance extends to various art forms, including music where many memorable songs featured individuals sporting a big Afro. The versatility of the Afro allows for numerous styling approaches, such as maintaining curls, incorporating shaved patterns, or opting for a flat top. Embrace the freedom of expression that comes with the Afro, and you'll enjoy the flexibility to switch between different styles whenever you desire.

  • Dreadlocks:

    • Dreadlocks have become a symbol of cultural expression and a popular hairstyle among men. Once you've mastered the art of creating and maintaining dreadlocks, you'll find that this style requires minimal daily styling. Dreadlocks offer a unique and striking appearance, making a bold statement about individuality and cultural identity. With a variety of lengths and sizes to choose from, you can customize your dreadlocks to suit your personal style and preferences.

  • Braid Styles:

    • Braids are not exclusive to individuals with shoulder-length hair; they can be a fantastic choice for men with various hair lengths. Whether you opt for classic cornrows or trendy fishtail braids, incorporating braids into your hairstyle adds texture and visual interest to your tresses. The versatility of braids allows you to experiment with different patterns and sizes, giving you the freedom to express your unique style. Men's hair is incredibly versatile, providing a range of styling options to suit different looks and preferences.

How Do You Make Natural Dreadlocks

Ready to make natural dreadlocks on your hair? Patience is key. The formation of locs varies from eight months to a year or more. In most cases, hair texture determines how fast dreadlocks form. Different hair textures will lock in different rates, therefore, the timeline varies from one person to another. For instance, loc-ing straight or wavy hair may even take up to two years. However, there are different techniques for the formation of locks. Determined by the hair type, hair length, lifestyle, and the size of the lock, each technique will give you the best results. Keep scrolling to check out step-by-step techniques to help you start this style.

  • The Twist and Rip Technique
    • Section Hair

      • Divide your hair into sections with uniform thicknesses and secure them with rubber bands.

    • Wash Your Hair

      • Wash your hair while rubbing it in one direction. It helps create the beginning of dreadlocks formation while creating the right texture.

    • Air Dry Your Hair

      • Begin drying the wet sections of the hair until it’s completely dry.

    • Roll Your Hair Using the Palm of Your Hands

      • You begin by ripping apart each tied-off section of the hair and applying wax to the section. Secondly, you roll the hair between your palm until it forms a dreadlock. However, to maintain defined dreads, you have to palm roll your hair each day. Also, it is advisable to not over-roll the hair since the locs become susceptible to disentangling. This technique is a more effective way to create locs by yourself!

  • The Backcombing Technique
    • Always Start with Clean Hair

      • Ensure that your hair is product-free and you can use shampoo and hair conditioner to remove residue from the hair. Also, you can moisturize your hair before drying naturally or using a blow dryer.

    • Create Sections to make Dreadlocks
    • You can section your hair from the front hairline or nape. Using your fingers or a comb, section your hair into square or triangle shapes with uniform sizes.

  • The Crochet Method

    • Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo and let it dry.
    • Section your hair according to the lock size and shape of your choice.
    • Backcomb the sectioned hair up.
    • Place your crochet hook between the hairs and pull. Make sure your hair is dry so that the crochet method will work.

    Crocheting works well for both textured and straight hair. In case you want to achieve an instant appearance of mature dreadlocks, then this one is for you.

    As you embark on your journey to achieve natural dreadlocks, hair will loosen throughout the days and months, and you will have to twist the loose strands of hair. It is also worth noting that performing loc maintenance methods will keep your style on point. Happy dreadlocking!
